Situation Update

The strategic landscape transformed in the 24 hours since yesterday's midday briefing — 1 day ago. US and Iranian negotiators announced a tentative memorandum of understanding to extend the ceasefire by 60 days, reopen the Strait of Hormuz with unrestricted commercial shipping and no tolls, and begin negotiations on Iran's nuclear program. The proposed MOU requires Iran to clear all mines from the Strait within 30 days and commits both sides to lifting their respective blockades proportionally as commercial traffic resumes. Iran would receive sanctions waivers enabling free oil sales — a direct economic lifeline for a regime Bessent described last week as unable to pay its troops. In return, Iran commits to never pursuing nuclear weapons, with the disposition of its estimated 440.9 kg of 60%-enriched uranium as the first negotiation topic.

Neither principal has signed. President Trump posted on social media that he would meet in the White House Situation Room to make a "final determination," and subsequently entered the meeting. From Tehran, Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei has not approved the draft; Iranian state media dismissed reports of a finalized deal as "incorrect." The critical structural contradiction: Khamenei issued a directive within the past week prohibiting enriched uranium from leaving Iran, while the MOU's first negotiation priority is precisely that removal. Iranian sources suggested dilution under IAEA supervision as an alternative — but not transfer abroad.
